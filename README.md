🛒 ShopZY — E-commerce Sales Optimization (Power BI)

🚀 Project Overview
This project simulates a real-world business scenario where ShopZY, a mid-sized e-commerce platform, struggles with:

Heavy reliance on discounts
Inconsistent regional performance
Lack of clarity on top-performing products

As a Business Analyst, I analyzed order-level data to uncover revenue leakages, growth opportunities, and strategic actions.

🎯 Business Objective

Identify:

Where revenue is being lost
Which regions and products drive growth
How to improve profitability without increasing customer acquisition cost

📊 Dataset Details
Type: Simulated e-commerce dataset
Size: 500 orders
Fields: Product, category, region, price, discount %, revenue, payment method, etc.

🔍 Key Insights (What Actually Matters)
💸 Discounts Are Killing Margins
₹21M gross sales → ₹18.73M net revenue
Profit drops to ₹6.17M
➡️ Excessive discounting is reducing profitability without clear ROI
🌍 South Region = Growth Engine
~₹5M revenue (highest contributor)
West region lags at ~₹3.8M
➡️ Strong regional imbalance = untapped potential
📦 No Clear Product Strategy
All major products contribute equally (~₹4–5M)
➡️ No focus = diluted marketing & inventory efforts
🧾 High Average Order Value (₹37K+)

➡️ Customers are already spending big — but:

No bundling
No upselling strategy
📉 Unpredictable Sales Trends

➡️ No seasonality or planning signals
➡️ Business is operating reactively

✅ Business Recommendations

✔️ Reduce Blind Discounting
Shift to targeted, data-driven discounts instead of blanket offers

✔️ Scale What’s Working (South Region)
Increase marketing & inventory where ROI is already proven

✔️ Fix Underperformance (West Region)
Investigate pricing, logistics, or awareness gaps

✔️ Focus on High-Impact Products
Prioritize 1–2 categories with best margin + demand

✔️ Increase Revenue per Customer
Introduce:

Bundling
Cross-sell recommendations

✔️ Stabilize Demand
Use:

Seasonal campaigns
Loyalty programs
Planned sale events

✔️ Promote Digital Payments
Encourage UPI/cards for faster, lower-cost transactions
