# 🛒 ShopZY — E-commerce Sales Optimization

> A business analysis project exploring how a mid-sized e-commerce platform can make smarter decisions around revenue, discounts, and regional performance — built using Power BI.

---

## 👋 About This Project

I built this project as part of my business analyst portfolio to practice the end-to-end BA workflow — from understanding a business problem all the way to delivering recommendations a real team could act on.

ShopZY is a fictional e-commerce company (think a smaller Amazon/Flipkart) that was struggling with inconsistent revenue across regions, heavy reliance on discounts, and no clear visibility into which products were actually driving the business. My job was to dig into the data and figure out what was going wrong — and what to do about it.

---

## 📊 The Dataset

- **Type:** Simulated order-level dataset
- **Size:** 500 orders
- **Fields covered:** Order ID, date, product, category, region, quantity, price, discount %, final price, and payment method

Since this is a simulated dataset, the absolute numbers are illustrative — but the analytical approach and the patterns uncovered reflect real business scenarios.

## 💡 Key Findings

Here's what stood out from the data:

**1. Margins are getting squeezed by discounts**
Total sales volume was ₹21M, but actual revenue came in at ₹18.73M — that gap is almost entirely discounting. And after costs, profit was only ₹6.17M. The business is working hard but not keeping much of what it earns.

**2. The South region is doing the heavy lifting**
South generates ~₹5M in revenue — the clear leader. Meanwhile, the West region is sitting at ~₹3.8M with no obvious reason for the gap. That's worth investigating.

**3. No hero product**
Tablets, Smartphones, Laptops, and Smartwatches all contribute roughly equally (~₹4–5M each). That sounds balanced, but it actually means there's no focused product strategy — the company is spreading effort evenly instead of doubling down on what works.

**4. Average Order Value is high — but untapped**
At ₹37.47K per order, customers are already spending a lot. That's a huge opportunity for upselling and bundling that the business isn't capitalizing on.

**5. Sales are unpredictable**
Monthly trends show peaks and drops with no clear seasonal pattern. This makes planning really difficult and suggests the business is reactive rather than strategic.

---

## ✅ My Recommendations

Based on the analysis, here's what I'd tell the ShopZY leadership team:

1. **Fix the discount strategy first** — Move from blanket discounts to targeted offers based on customer segments and purchase history. Protecting margins is more sustainable than chasing volume with discounts.

2. **Double down on the South region** — It's already the strongest market. More marketing investment and inventory availability here will give the fastest ROI.

3. **Investigate and fix the West region** — The revenue gap between South and West suggests either low brand awareness, logistics issues, or poor pricing competitiveness. A region-specific campaign could close that gap.

4. **Build a product focus strategy** — Identify the 1–2 products with the best margin and demand profile, and build campaigns around those rather than promoting everything equally.

5. **Leverage the high AOV with upselling** — Introduce product bundling and cross-sell recommendations to push AOV even higher without acquiring new customers.

6. **Create predictable demand with seasonal campaigns** — Introduce loyalty programs, subscription offers, and planned sale events to smooth out the revenue spikes and dips.

7. **Nudge customers toward digital payments** — With UPI and cards offering cashback incentives, shifting customers away from cash speeds up transactions and reduces handling costs.

## 🖥️ The Dashboard

The Power BI dashboard covers all of the above through:

- **KPI Cards** — Revenue, profit, AOV, and total orders at a glance
- **Monthly Sales Trend** — Line chart showing how revenue moves over time
- **Regional Revenue Breakdown** — Column chart comparing all regions
- **Product Contribution** — Pie and donut charts showing category and product split
- **Discount Impact Funnel** — Visual showing how discounting affects final revenue
- **Slicers** — Filter everything by region, product, or payment method
